About the Breakup Budget Separation Calculator

Going through a separation is emotionally challenging. This calculator helps you navigate the financial side by comparing your combined budget with individual post-separation budgets, dividing assets and debts, and identifying financial gaps that need to be addressed.

How to use this separation calculator

  • Enter your current combined income, expenses, and debts.
  • Enter each partner's projected individual income and expenses after separation.
  • List assets and debts to divide, and one-time separation costs.
  • Calculate to see each person's financial standing and recommended next steps.

Breakup Budget Separation FAQs

Common questions about managing finances during separation.

Why should we plan our budget during a breakup?

Separation is financially disruptive. Planning helps you understand your new financial reality, identify shortfalls, and create a realistic path forward. It reduces stress and helps both partners transition more smoothly.

How do we split shared expenses fairly?

The calculator shows both equal and income-based splits. Consider what's fair for your situation — sometimes 50/50 works, other times proportional by income is more equitable, especially if there's a significant income gap.

What assets should we include in the division?

Include cash, investments, retirement accounts, property equity, vehicles, and other valuable assets. The calculator has categories for most common assets. Consult a professional for complex assets.

How do we handle shared debt?

Shared debt should be divided just like assets. Consider who incurred the debt and for what purpose. The calculator includes a combined debt category that can be split using the same method as assets.

What are "one-time separation costs"?

These include moving costs, legal fees, new furniture, deposits on new housing, and other expenses you'll only incur once during the separation process. Budgeting for these helps avoid surprises.

What if one partner has significantly less income?

The calculator highlights income gaps. Consider whether temporary support, asset division adjustments, or lifestyle changes might be needed to ensure both partners can live sustainably.

Should we use equal or proportional asset splitting?

Equal splitting is common and simple. Proportional by income can be fairer when incomes differ significantly. Custom percentage gives you flexibility if you have specific agreements in mind.

What if we have a mortgage together?

The mortgage is both a debt and an asset (property). Include the property equity in assets and the mortgage in debts. Decide whether to sell the property or if one partner will buy out the other's share.

How do we handle joint accounts?

Joint accounts should be frozen or closed during separation. Divide the balance according to your agreed split method. The calculator shows you the amount each partner would receive from joint savings.

What about retirement accounts?

Retirement accounts may need a Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO) to divide legally. Include them in investments/retirement assets. Consult a professional for legal division.

Should we include child-related expenses?

Yes, child-related expenses are critical. Include them in each partner's individual budget based on custody arrangements. The calculator helps you see if child support is needed to balance budgets.

What if our separation is amicable?

Even in amicable separations, financial planning is important. This calculator helps you maintain fairness and avoid future disputes by making the numbers transparent and clear.

How do we handle tax implications?

Tax implications vary by jurisdiction. This calculator focuses on monthly budgeting and asset division. Consult a tax professional for advice on your specific situation.

What if one partner wants to keep the home?

Include the property equity in assets and the mortgage in debts. The partner keeping the home may need to "buy out" the other partner's share. The calculator shows the equity amount to be divided.

How do we deal with different spending habits?

After separation, each person manages their own spending. The calculator assumes each partner will adjust their lifestyle to their new budget. It highlights if a budget seems unrealistic given past spending patterns.

What if we have pets?

Pet expenses should be included in individual budgets for the partner who keeps the pet. If sharing custody, divide expenses accordingly. The calculator's "other expenses" category can include pet costs.

Is this calculator legally binding?

No, this is a planning tool, not legal advice. Use it as a starting point for discussions. For legal separation agreements, consult a professional.

Can this calculator handle multiple assets?

Yes, the calculator aggregates assets into categories. For complex situations with many assets, you may want to use a spreadsheet for detailed tracking and then enter totals into the categories provided.

How often should we review our separation budget?

Review monthly for the first 6 months, then quarterly. Budgets often need adjustment as you settle into new living arrangements and expenses become clearer.

Is this calculator free to use?

Yes, completely free. We believe financial clarity during difficult times should be accessible. The calculator is supported by ads to keep it free.

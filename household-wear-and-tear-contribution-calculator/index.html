About the Wear-and-Tear Contribution Calculator

Everything in a household experiences wear and tear — from carpets and appliances to furniture and plumbing. This calculator helps households fairly plan for replacement and maintenance costs by spreading them over time and allocating them fairly among residents.

How to use this calculator

  • Enter household members and their income (for income-based allocation).
  • Add items with replacement cost, lifespan, and who uses them.
  • Choose allocation method (equal, by income, by usage, or custom).
  • Add depreciation and inflation rates for more accurate planning.
  • Calculate to see each person's fair contribution.

Wear-and-Tear FAQs

Common questions about household wear-and-tear planning.

Why plan for wear and tear?

Everything in your home eventually needs repair or replacement. Planning for these costs prevents financial surprises and ensures fair distribution of expenses that benefit everyone.

What's the difference between depreciation and wear and tear?

Depreciation is the financial value loss over time. Wear and tear is the physical deterioration from use. This calculator uses both concepts to determine fair contributions for replacement.

How is usage factor calculated?

Usage factor reflects how much someone uses household items. A factor of 1.2 means they use 20% more than average. This is useful when one person works from home or has different habits.

Should we include appliances in wear and tear?

Yes, appliances like refrigerators, washing machines, and dishwashers experience wear and tear and need replacement. Include them with their expected lifespan.

What about furniture?

Furniture is a major category. Sofas, beds, chairs, and tables all experience wear from daily use. Include them in the calculator.

How do we handle flooring and carpets?

Flooring wears down over time, especially in high-traffic areas. Include replacement costs and expected lifespan. Different rooms may have different usage.

What's a reasonable lifespan for appliances?

Typical lifespans: Refrigerators 10-15 years, washing machines 10-13 years, dishwashers 8-12 years, ovens 10-15 years. Adjust based on quality and usage.

Should we include inflation in our planning?

Yes, inflation increases replacement costs over time. Including a modest inflation rate (2-4%) gives a more realistic long-term plan.

How often should we review our wear-and-tear plan?

Review annually or when major items are replaced. The plan should evolve as your household changes.

What if someone moves out?

Re-run the calculator with updated members. The contribution amounts will adjust to reflect who's still using the items.

Should we include exterior maintenance?

Yes, if it applies to shared spaces. Include items like outdoor furniture, garden tools, and exterior paint.

How do we handle items used by only one person?

Assign them to that person in the "Used By" field. They'll pay the full cost of those items.

What about shared items like a communal TV?

Mark them as "Shared" in the "Used By" field. The cost will be split according to your chosen method.

Can this calculator handle more than 2 people?

Yes! Use the "Add Person" button to add as many household members as you need.

Is this calculator free to use?

Yes, completely free. We believe fair household planning should be accessible to everyone.

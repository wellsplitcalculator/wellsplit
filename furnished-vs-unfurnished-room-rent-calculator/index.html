About the Furnished vs Unfurnished Room Rent Calculator

This calculator helps roommates account for furniture provided with certain bedrooms when dividing monthly rent.

Each room receives a base value from its size and optional share of common space. Partly furnished rooms receive half of the selected premium, while fully furnished rooms receive the full premium.

How to use it

  • Enter the monthly rent and room measurements.
  • Choose whether shared space should be included.
  • Set the furnished-room premium.
  • Add each room and its occupants.
  • Select the furnishing level for each room.
  • Calculate room and per-person payments.

Only furniture supplied with the rental or by the household should affect the calculation. Furniture personally owned by a roommate should not increase their rent.

Furnished vs Unfurnished Room FAQs

Helpful answers about furniture and roommate rent.

What is a furnished vs unfurnished room rent calculator?

It helps roommates divide rent when some bedrooms include furniture and others do not. Furnished rooms can receive a higher share because they provide extra value and convenience.

How does the calculator work?

Enter the rent, room sizes, occupants, shared-space settings, and the furnished-room premium. The calculator adjusts the value of furnished rooms before dividing the rent.

What counts as a furnished room?

A furnished room usually includes essential items such as a bed, mattress, dresser, desk, chair, or wardrobe provided for the occupant.

What counts as an unfurnished room?

An unfurnished room has little or no essential furniture provided. The occupant normally supplies their own bed, storage, desk, and other items.

How much more should a furnished room cost?

There is no universal amount. A premium of 5% to 20% may be reasonable depending on the furniture's quality, completeness, and condition.

Is the furnished premium added directly to rent?

No. It increases the furnished room's effective value. The calculator then redistributes the full rent across all rooms.

Should old furniture receive the same premium?

Not necessarily. Use a lower premium when the furniture is worn, incomplete, or of limited value.

Should luxury furniture receive a higher premium?

It can. A higher premium may be reasonable for high-quality furniture, a complete bedroom set, or a dedicated desk and workspace.

What if only some furniture is included?

Choose Partly furnished. The calculator applies half of the selected furnished-room premium.

Can a couple use a furnished room?

Yes. Enter the couple's room once and set the occupants to two. The calculator will show both the room total and the per-person payment.

Can multiple people share one room?

Yes. Enter the correct number of occupants. The room total will be divided equally among them.

Can room size also affect rent?

Yes. Room size provides the base value, while furniture adds an adjustment on top of that value.

Can shared space be included?

Yes. Shared space can be excluded, divided equally by room, or divided according to occupants.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Use the same measurement unit for all bedrooms and shared-space entries.

What if all rooms are furnished?

If all rooms have the same furnishing level, furniture will not create major differences. Room size and shared-space allocation will drive the result.

What if furniture belongs to the roommate?

Do not mark the room as furnished by the rental or household. Personal furniture should not increase that roommate's rent.

Does this include utilities?

No. It calculates monthly rent only. Utilities can be divided separately.

Do all room totals add up to the full rent?

Yes. The calculator distributes the full monthly rent across all rooms, apart from minor display rounding.

Can I copy the result?

Yes. Use the Copy Result button after calculating.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is an estimate for roommate discussions. Everyone should agree to the final rent split.

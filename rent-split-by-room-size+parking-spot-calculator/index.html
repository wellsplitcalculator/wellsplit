About the Room Size + Parking Spot Rent Calculator

The WellSplit Rent Split by Room Size + Parking Spot Calculator helps roommates divide rent when bedrooms differ in size and one or more roommates receive reserved parking.

Each room receives a base value from its bedroom size and assigned share of common space. Rooms with reserved parking receive the premium selected above. Rent is then distributed according to each room's effective value.

How to use this calculator

  • Enter the total monthly rent and choose a currency.
  • Select square feet or square metres.
  • Enter shared-space size and choose how it should be divided.
  • Choose a parking-spot premium.
  • Add each room, its size, occupants, and number of parking spots.
  • Calculate each room total and per-person amount.

This method provides a clear starting point for homes with unequal bedrooms, reserved parking, couples, and shared rooms. Everyone should review and agree to the final rent split.

Room Size + Parking Spot Rent Split FAQs

These answers explain how room size, reserved parking, shared space, and occupancy can affect a fair roommate rent split.

What is a rent split by room size and parking spot calculator?

It helps roommates divide rent using bedroom size and parking access. Larger rooms and rooms with reserved parking can receive a higher share of the rent.

How does the calculator work?

Enter the total rent, room sizes, occupants, shared-space settings, and the parking premium. The calculator assigns each room an effective value and divides rent proportionally.

How should a parking spot affect rent?

A reserved parking spot can increase a room's value, especially in areas where parking is limited or expensive. The calculator lets the household choose the size of that premium.

What parking premium should I use?

There is no universal amount. Many households use a premium between 5% and 20%, depending on local parking value, convenience, security, and exclusivity.

Is the parking premium a percentage of the whole rent?

No. It increases the value of the room or roommate group receiving the parking spot. The final rent share is then calculated from all room values together.

How is room size used in the calculation?

Each room's size is compared with the total bedroom area. Larger bedrooms receive a larger base share before any parking premium is applied.

Can I include shared space?

Yes. You can enter the total shared-space size and divide it equally by room, according to occupants, or exclude it.

What counts as shared space?

Shared space normally includes the kitchen, living room, hallways, laundry area, and shared bathrooms.

What if one roommate has one parking spot?

Mark that room as having one parking spot. The selected premium will increase that room's effective value.

What if one room has two parking spots?

Choose two parking spots for that room. The calculator applies the selected premium for each spot.

What if parking is shared or unassigned?

Do not assign a parking premium to any room. Shared or first-come parking should generally be treated as a common benefit.

Can couples use this calculator?

Yes. Enter the couple's room as one room and set the occupants to two. The calculator will show the room total and per-person amount.

Can multiple people share one bedroom?

Yes. Enter the room once and set the correct number of occupants. The room total will be divided equally among them.

Can I use square feet or square metres?

Yes. Use either square feet or square metres, but keep the same unit for all bedrooms and shared-space measurements.

How do I measure a bedroom?

Measure the room's length and width and multiply them. For irregular rooms, divide the space into smaller rectangles and add their areas.

What if all bedrooms are the same size?

Rooms without parking will pay the same amount. A room with a reserved parking spot will pay more according to the selected premium.

Does the calculator include parking fees charged separately?

No. It adjusts the rent split based on parking value. If the lease lists a separate parking fee, you may prefer to assign that fee directly to the parking user.

Does this calculator include utilities?

No. It calculates monthly rent only. Utilities can be divided separately.

Do all room totals add up to the full rent?

Yes. The full monthly rent is distributed across all entered rooms, subject only to minor display rounding.

Is the result legally binding?

No. It is an estimate for roommate discussions. Everyone should agree to the final split and follow the lease.
